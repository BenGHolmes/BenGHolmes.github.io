Autonomous Robot

Course Project

June - August 2018

Fully autonomous rescue robot. Uses computer vision to find and retrieve stuffed animals in a complex and dynamic environment.

The Problem

Four Ewoks and Chewbacca are trapped in an Empire Stronghold! We need to build an autonomous robot to go retrieve them, or at least retrieve more than our classmates so we win the competition. Doing so involves navigating over gaps, ramps and step-ups, responding to different IR frequencies on the course (to avoid the storm troopers), and finally spotting and retrieving the prisoners.

The diagram above is of the competition surface and shows the positions of the Ewoks and Chewbacca, the gaps that we had to cross, and the position and direction of the IR sensors.

Mechanical Design

WIDBAT. When In Doubt, Build A Tank. And that's exactly what we did. We took our inspiration from WWI tanks crossing trenches, and angled the front of our tracks so we could climb over the gaps and step-ups. The final product was a beast. We used four, geared-down DC motors PER SIDE, to give us the torque needed to pull the weight of the chassis, batteries and electronics over the step-up. These motors were mounted in between two sheets of aluminum that sat inside the tracks, which freed up the space between the tracks for all of the batteries and electronics. Towards the front sat the polycarbonate arm and camera mount. Finally, our basket sat on top with a hook that could be connected to the zip line after rescuing Chewbacca; returning our friends to safety.

Software

To navigate towards the Ewoks, we used a camera, a Raspberry Pi, and an implementation of the YOLO algorithm for object localization. The rest of the robot was controlled by an STM32 micro-controller, which was responsible for the PID motor control, measuring the IR beacon, edge detection, and actuating the claw and basket.
